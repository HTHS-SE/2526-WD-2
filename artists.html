Artists

Getting to know Impressionist artists

Claude Monet

1840-1926


Claude Monet was a French painter during the Impressionist movement. Some of his famous paintings include: Impression, Sunrise; The Water Lily Pond; Woman with a Parasol, Garden at Sainte-Adresse. The term impressionism is based on his painting Impression, Sunrise.

Claude Monet

Oscar-Claude Monet was born in November 1840 in Paris, France. Despite his father wanting him to be a part of the family grocery store business, Monet aspired to be an artist at a young age. He attended Le Havre secondary school of the arts at the age of 10. 6 years later, his mom died and he moved in with his aunt.

When Monet was 21, he joined the First Regiment of African Light Cavalry in Algeria. His aunt pressured him to end his service and complete an art course at a university. There, he became a student of Charles Gleyre and met many other famous artists. They include Pierre-Auguste Renoir, Alfred Sisley, and Frédéric Bazille. All of the aspiring artists shared a new approach for art.

The Franco-Prussian war broke out which impacted the lives of many artists such as Monet. He and his wife at the time ended up taking refuge in England, where he studied the works of others. However, his most popular paintings were created around the time of when his wife died in 1879.

Around 13 years later, he married Alicia Hoschede. Their family moved to Giverny, France where Monet felt very inspired. Paintings such as the Water Lilies, the Houses of Parliament, Mornings on the Seine, and more. In 1923, cataracts started to form on Monet’s eyes. This impacted the hues and colors of the paintings. Monet ended up passing away on December 5th 1926 due to lung cancer.

Camille Pissarro

1830-1903


Camille Pissarro was a pivotal figure in the Impressionist movement. His works were known for their depictions of everyday life and landscape. Pissarro’s art pieces include The Crystal Palace, The Boulevard Montmartre at Night, the Apple Harvest, etc.

Camille Pissarro

Camille Pissarro was born on July 10 1830 in the US Virgin Islands. He lived in St. Thomas until he was 12 years old and then went to boarding school in Paris for a little bit. Ever since Pissarro was young, he drew in his free time. A Danish painter, Fritz Melbye, inspired Pissarro to paint full time. He became both his teacher and his friend. When Pissarro was 25, he went to Paris again to study under more artists.

Pissarro married Julie Vellay and had 8 children, 6 of them ended up becoming painters. During the Franco-Prussian War, their family had to flee France. They took refuge in Westow Hill, London. Here, Pissarro continued to create oil paintings. The paintings depicted scenery from Central London.

In 1890, Pissarro returned back to France. His work focused on rural and urban French life. It contained scenes from everyday life and landscapes. It is also interesting to note He also mentored many known artists, including Pierre-Auguste Renoir.

Pissarro played a crucial role in developing Impressionist techniques and theories. He passed away on November 13 1909.

Pierre-Auguste Renior

1830-1903


Pierre-Auguste Renoir was a French Impressionist painter who focused on the human figure and everyday life. His works include the Luncheon of the Boating Party , Dance at Bougival, The Large Bathers, etc.

Pierre Auguste Renior

Pierre-Auguste Renoir was born on February 25th, 1841, in France. As a child, he worked in a porcelain factory. Renoir painted designs on fine china and hangings/decorations on fans. He would also visit the Louvre to study the works of French artists. In 1862, he began to study art under Charles Gleyre. Renoir became acquainted with Claude Monet, Alfred Sisley, and Frédéric Bazille. During his time in art school, he did not have enough money to buy paint. This was why he started to exhibit his work. Renoir did not gain recognition right away due to the Franco-Prussian War.

At this point in his career, his subjects consisted of scenic places and nature. As he lost access to his inspiration, the Fontainebleau, his subjects started to change. He also began to paint portraits of people. The people painted, mostly women, were depicted to be more beautiful and elegant.

Renoir continued to travel to see the art created by different artists from different countries. He went to Algeria, Spain, Italy, England, etc. During his summer in Guernsey (one of the Channel Islands in the English Channel), he created 15 paintings featuring different aspects of nature. In 1887, Renoir was able to donate some of his paintings to Queen Victoria for her Golden Jubilee.

3 years later, Renoir married Aline Victorine Charigot. She served as a model for one of his previous paintings, the Luncheon of the Boating Party (1881). The couple ended up having 3 sons together. As a result, Renoir started to paint many scenes of his everyday family life. Renoir continued to paint until he was old. Even when he got arthritis, he painted. The artist sadly passed away on December 3rd, 1919.

Alfred Sisley

1830-1903


Alfred Sisley was a popular English artist who focused on depicting landscapes. He became one of the most consistent Impressionist artists, despite financial hardships and little recognition.

Alfred Sisley

Alfred Sisley was born on October 30th, 1839, in France to British parents. His father was in the silk business, while his mother was a music connoisseur. When Sisley was 18 years old, he went to London to study business. He felt that this was not for him and returned to Paris. In 1862, he studied at Marc-Charles-Gabriel Gleyre’s art studio. He met Claude Monet, Pierre-Auguste Renoir, and Frederic Bazille. The group painted landscapes together outside to capture the sunlight’s effects properly. Many paintings created with this technique were more colorful than previous creations.

Sisley mainly focused on landscape painting. He depicts a serene and calming environment in his paintings. Unlike many artists, Sisley received an allowance from his father. At this time, he just painted as he pleased. However, when his father’s business failed, Sisley had to rely on the sale of his works to earn a living. From this point on, Sisley lived in poverty, as his paintings gained popularity only after his death. This impacted Sisley drastically because he was not used to this lifestyle.

In 1866, he married Eugenie Lesouezee, and their family lived in France. The couple ended up having two children. Even with all his challenges, Sisley was still committed to his career as a painter. He depicted places near Paris, including Versailles, and the forests at Mali. Due to the pressure in his financial situation, Sisley ran into some issues with his art dealer. Although he did not achieve the same amount of success as his colleagues, Sisley maintained a positive mentality.

During his last few years, he was in Wales. There, he painted various seascapes. Sisley passed away on January 29th, 1899. It is interesting to note that he asked his friend, Monet, to take care of his kids.
